The Process

Inside a Session

Every session is structured. Every throw has a purpose. Here's what a lesson looks like from start to finish.

01

Dynamic Warmup

5–6 min

We start every session the same way — getting the body ready to throw. Light jog, shuffles, high knees, heel flicks, knee pulls, leg swings, walking lunges with rotation. This is how pro pitchers warm up. It becomes routine.

02

Arm Warmup

5–6 min

Shoulder rolls, arm circles, prisoner stretch, backstroke motion, goal post stretch, band pull-aparts, and external rotations. We're preparing the arm to throw — not just going through the motions. Every pitcher learns this routine and can do it on their own before games.

03

Throwing Progression

10–14 min

We build up distance and intensity gradually: wrist flicks at 15–20 ft, planted throws at 30–40 ft, rocker throws at 40–50 ft, then stretch it out to long toss. Every throw is on a line with good backspin — if the ball starts bouncing more than once, we come back in 10 ft. Long toss is how you build arm strength safely.

04

Bullpen / Mound Work

10–15 min

This is where the real work happens. 10–15 tracked pitches recording strikes and balls. Radar gun for velocity baseline. Individual mechanic cues — things like back leg drive, hip loading, mound presence. I'm watching delivery, tempo, composure, and the routine between pitches. Quality over quantity — we stop if mechanics start to break down.

05

Mechanic Cues

Throughout

Every pitcher walks away with 2–4 simple cues they can actually remember. Things like: lead with the front hip, load the back leg, legs throw — arm delivers, same thing every time. No overload. Just clear, repeatable cues built around what they need most.

06

Recovery + Cool Down

5–8 min

Band pull-aparts, upward heavy ball tosses, internal/external rotations, planks, and follow-through balance drills. We take care of the arm after we use it. This is how you stay healthy and throw all season.

01

Warmup + Isolation Swings

5–8 min

Light jog, shuffles, stretching, then top hand and bottom hand isolation swings with a short bat. This wakes up the hands and helps feel the swing path before we pick up a full bat.

02

Tee Work Progression

15–20 min

We build the swing from the ground up with a structured tee progression: stop at contact to check hand position, stop at extension to feel the drive through the ball, feet closed swings to isolate rotation, slide step to add weight shift, leg kick / flamingo to engage the lower half, step-over swings for hip drive, then full swings with intent. Each drill builds on the last. High tee, low tee — adjusting barrel path for different pitch locations.

03

Front Toss

8–10 min

Now we add timing and ball tracking. The player carries over what he built on the tee and reacts to the ball without overthinking. This is the bridge between drill work and game swings.

04

Live BP

10–12 min

Game-like reps. Put everything together. Swing hard — don't aim. The work is in the drills; when it's time to swing, let it go. This is where we measure exit velocity and see how the mechanical work translates under real conditions.

05

Fielding Drills

8–10 min (60-min sessions)

We close with a quick fielding circuit: self-bounce / backhand / side scoop, glove work with hands out front, flamingo lateral footwork for range, chop feet drills to the backhand side, and turn-two footwork. Quick, practical reps that translate straight to game situations. Great as a pre-game routine.

01

Group Warmup

18–20 min

All players together. Dynamic warmup, arm warmup, then arm activation drills — left/right hand pitching moves, bicep band curls, shoulder perturbations, bat-behind-back and bat-over-top motions. Builds body awareness and shoulder stability before anyone touches a baseball.

02

Throwing Progression

12–14 min

Pair up. Wrist flicks, planted throws, rocker throws, ball-under-armpit throws, football-hold throws to feel front-side pull, then build to long toss. Everyone throws together, building distance and intent at the same pace.

03

Bullpen Rotation

25–30 min

Two pitchers on the mound at a time while the other two catch and shag. Groups swap after each round. Each pitcher gets 5–10 warmup pitches, 10–15 tracked pitches (recording strikes and balls), and 3–5 max-effort velocity readings. I'm tracking strike %, velocity, mechanic cues, and mound presence for every player individually.

04

Recovery + Individual Reports

10–15 min

Cool down together with band work, heavy ball deceleration tosses, core holds, and balance drills. After the clinic, every player gets their own evaluation report with personal baselines, mechanic notes, and a throwing program — same deliverables as a private lesson.

What Every Player Walks Away With

01

Evaluation Report

Detailed session recap sent after every lesson. Includes velocity range, average velocity, strike %, total pitches, mechanic observations, main focus area, positives, and specific homework. For hitters: exit velocity, throw velocity, swing mechanics breakdown, and key adjustments.

02

Throwing Program

A 3-day-per-week program built around the player's current arm speed. Includes a full pre-throw warmup routine, weekly schedule (catch play, mechanical work days, bullpen/target days), an 8-week long toss distance progression, pitch count guidelines, and the mechanic cues from the session.

03

Drill Menus

Take-home drill sheets with the exact drills we did in the session. For hitters: the full tee progression and fielding routine with reps and focus cues. For pitchers: arm care exercises, band work, and deceleration drills they can do on off days.

The Approach

How I Coach

Elite Knowledge

Everything I teach comes from what I learned playing at the highest levels — San Jac, Rice, the College World Series, professional ball. I'm not guessing. I'm passing down what was passed down to me by some of the best coaches in the game.

Calm Delivery

I don't yell. I don't make kids feel small. I coach the way the best coaches coached me — clear, direct, and with respect. Kids learn faster when they're not playing scared. My job is to build confidence, not break it down.

Real Development

Every session has a plan. Every player gets measurable data. Velocity. Strike percentage. Mechanic cues they can actually remember and work on. This isn't daycare with a batting cage — it's structured development with a purpose.

The Underdog Mentality

I came up through the grind — select ball in Houston, junior college, earning a spot at Rice, grinding through pro ball. I know what it takes to compete when you're not the biggest name in the room. That mentality is baked into everything I teach.

“Calm, cool, collected coaching. Push ’em to get better — don’t break ’em down.”
